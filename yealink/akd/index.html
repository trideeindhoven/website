AKD

In the fall of 2023 i was summoned by Dutch lawyer firm AKD. The name of this legal firm is relevant because this is not a small legal firm. AKD has build itself quiet a reputation. In 2022 research by Follow The Money showed that AKD did not distance itself from the Kremlin. I was highly surprised i received a summon from a lawfirm that, until recently, was working for Russian state-owned companies that were involved in the nuclear industry. The summon was very clear: AKD’s client Lydis was loosing money fast because of the publication in Follow The Money. The summon was very specific about the amount of money lost by Lydis. It mentioned an amount of 7 million euro and if i was not going to be very careful this amount was going to be collected at…me!

This is an important aspect because if i had to pay 7 million euro to Lydis this would mean i was going to be homeless and my life was going to be ruined. Apparently the stakes for Lydis were this high that they were willing to do that to a Dutch security researcher who had, for free, worked with them for a year to point out several security issues. I think this should be a major factor if you want to do business with a company that is doing sustainable business. It is hard to maintain a sustainable image if you want to ruin someone’s life for asking, arguably, difficult questions.
